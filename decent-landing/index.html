What you actually get

Not marketing fluff. Real properties that change who controls communication.

transport

Peer-to-peer delivery

Messages travel between peers over encrypted data channels instead of a permanent central inbox.

crypto

Forward secrecy

Session keys rotate. Exposure of a current key does not unlock historical conversation history.

identity

BIP39 seed identity

Your cryptographic identity derives from a mnemonic phrase you control and can back up independently.

reliability

Offline + sync recovery

Local persistence and sync logic let peers catch up after disconnects without losing conversation continuity.

governance

Open protocol evolution

Changes are tracked in DEPs, so architecture shifts are explicit, reviewable, and testable.

product

PWA-ready UX

Runs in the browser, installable, responsive, and usable without forcing app-store distribution.

architecture / simplified

How a new conversation starts

  • Generate identity from mnemonic phrase (deterministic keys).
  • Exchange invite metadata through signaling path.
  • Establish WebRTC data channel between peers.
  • Encrypt payloads with ratcheting session keys.
  • Merge state with CRDT/vector-clock rules on reconnect.
security model

Threat model, not trust model

  • No default central history bucket to harvest.
  • Cryptography is in protocol flow, not optional settings.
  • Source is inspectable; behavior can be verified.
  • Identity and keys stay user-controlled by design.
  • Security posture improves through DEP-driven iteration.
